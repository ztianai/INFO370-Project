Focus

Our project focused on the mapping of legal and illegal transactions made through Bitcoin, a modern cryptocurrency and peer-to-peer payment system.

Question

"Can we determine the legality and places of high volume in illegal Bitcoin transactions using crime statistics and information about the Darknet?"

Approach

We desired to predict information about the buyer, seller, and exchange of goods in anonymous Bitcoin transactions by cross referencing a number of related datasets. By creating a network of blockchain nodes, we were able to correlate flows of transactions with crime data to link these transactions with exchanges of goods. We are aware of the sensitive nature of this kind of data, and continue to emphasize that our presentation of all data is representative and accurate, without risking personal privacy violations of involved parties.

To analyze and map out Bitcoin transactions, we need a multifaceted approach to trend-spotting. We specifically looked into three areas of data, including the Blockchain, Darknet forums, and national crime data.

Data

Firstly, we used the data from the Bitcoin Blockchain API to analyze Bitcoin transactions and wallet data in search of patterns. The blockchain nodes provided IP addresses that allowed us to approximate the pathway of a transaction.

Secondly, we used this Darknet dataset generated by an individual who scraped most of the popular Darknet forums and marketplaces starting for data about volume and patterns of sellers and goods sold.

And lastly, we used this FBI crime dataset to analyze the numbers of various criminal arrests by geo-location, some of which might could have relations to illegal Bitcoin transactions on the Darknet.

Results

We produced visualizations to represent real-time exchanges and predict spending behaviors. The visualizations include an interactive timeline of market histories and a graph explorer of Blockchain nodes. The goal of the website is to inform people of the ways that Bitcoin is spent, and the scale of the Bitcoin economy.

Next Steps

We believe our project has boldly scratched the surface of researching a largely unanalyzed cryptocurrency. With further resources, many areas can, and should be further explored. This includes examining the data sets for Bitcoin tumbling (laundering), legitimacy of transactions, prediction of market prices and inflation of this highly volatile currency, patterns in types of markets (i.e. drugs, fake documents, services, donations), further analysis of specific events, such as marketplace openings and closings.

Due to resource constraints, we were able to conduct analysis only on small datasets from specific timelines. That being said, we hope that our project can provide insights for future generalizable research and predictive ability in this field. It can also serve as a foundation for further exploration of how these transactions relate or affect other illegal transaction patterns, ideally, utilizing the wealth of drug and crime data available.
